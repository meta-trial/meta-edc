Topic Guide for Withdrawals

1. Demographics: Age, gender, site

How long were you involved in the META trial?

2. Can you tell me about your overall experience in the META trial?

(If at least for 2-3 months, then ask the highlighted questions below)
    What did you understand about the purpose of the META trial?
  • What motivated you to participate?
  • What aspects did you like or dislike?

3. What factors influenced your decision to stop participating?

4. Have you been involved in a similar study before?

  • If yes, how did your experience in the META trial compare?

5. Have you noticed any improvements in your health since joining the trial?

  • Were there any changes before leaving the trial? After leaving the trial

6. Did participating in the trial lead to changes in your health behaviours?

#(If at least for 2-3 months, then ask the highlighted questions below)
  • How did you feel about the lifestyle advice given (e.g., diet, physical activity)?
  • Did you adapt the advice to suit your personal circumstances?
  • What were your eating habits like before and during the trial? Did they change?
  • Did you engage in any physical activity? If yes, can you describe it?
  • Any other reasons why the trial might have changed your health behaviour?
  • What were there barriers that prevented you from following the advice fully?

7. Did you experience side effects from the trial or medication? If yes, can you describe what these side effects were like?

8. How did you take the medication during the trial?

(If they have taken med 2-3 months, then ask the highlighted questions below)
  • How often did you take it? Was it easy to remember?
  • Did it fit into your daily routine?
  • What challenges, if any, made it difficult to take the medication?
  • What kind of support would have helped with adherence?

Did any of these challenges a factor in your decision?

1. If the intervention drug proves effective, would you be willing to continue using it?

  • For how long? What factors might prevent you from continuing?
  • what would influence your willingness to use it in the future? Whats needed?

What improvements would you suggest for future trials like this?

  • What advice would you give to future participants?

2. What do you think are the main barriers to accessing healthcare for people with prediabetes, especially related to social factors?

3. Would you recommend a trial like this to family or friends?

  • What would influence your decision to encourage or discourage them?

4. Is there any other feedback you'd like to share (positive or negative)?
